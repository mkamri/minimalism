minimalism the ultimate beginner’s guide

let’s make this simple.

As more and more people enjoy the life-changing benefits of simplicity, our simple-living online space has gotten cluttered with opinions and arguments about the “best” way to be a minimalist.

I'm here to simplify all that information into a one-stop resource to help you launch your very own minimalist journey. I’ll also be linking some popular blogs, books and articles along the way for further reading.

defining minimalism:

  1. A minimalist is someone without excess who focuses on what matters to them.
  2. You get to decide what minimalism means for you, so long as you live intentionally.
  3. Minimalism is a personal commitment to simplicity, nothing more and nothing less. 

three steps to minimalist freedom

1: find your why

Search within yourself. What do you want from simplifying? What does your dream life look like?

2: get rid of junk

Get rid of the stuff that’s consuming your time, energy, and money. Make room for what’s truly important.

3: infinity and beyond

Discover time and energy you could have never dreamed of. Learn what your true values are. Experience the satisfaction of finally being enough.

step onefind your why

why minimalism?

If you’ve ever read a self-help book, you’ve probably heard of the importance of why-power. Having a firm grasp on why you’re doing something is crucial to sticking with it when things get tough.

Minimalism may be simple, but it isn’t easy at first. There will be moments when you question yourself, when you relapse, or when you want to give up entirely. In these difficult times, having a journal entry or a word document reminding you of your goals is paramount.

common why's:

My house is a mess.

I feel like I’m constantly cleaning, but nothing seems to stay clean for long. I feel anxious or exhausted when I come home and see the state of things. I hate doing huge loads of laundry. The dishes seem to be forever dirty. I lose things often. I want to enjoy my living space.

I keep buying things I don’t need.

I’m in debt, but for some reason I keep buying things I know I don’t need. I run out of money by the end of the month and don’t know where it goes. I keep finding excuses to go shopping and it’s affecting my relationships and my mental health. I want to heal my finances.

I’m always distracted.

I feel like my brain is jumbled up. I have trouble focusing on one thing at a time. I have so much I need to do every day and I can’t seem to find time for my personal goals. I have so many projects that I don’t end up working on any of them. I want to become productive.

take action

Write down what you are dissatisfied with in your current life. This can be anything, including things that seem unrelated to minimalism.

Use this dissatisfaction list to determine what you wish your life looked like. This could be a dream schedule (I wish my day looked like…), a mood wishlist (I wish my life was relaxing, adventurous, productive, etc), or anything else you’d like more of in your life.

Get creative! Write yourself a letter for when you get frustrated and want to give up. Draw a picture of your dream living room. Make a pinterest board of some minimalist spaces that inspire you. Do whatever you need to get yourself motivated to get started, and to keep you going through the hard days.
